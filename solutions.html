Sustainable & Eco-friendly Practices

Integrated Disease Management (IDM)

Combine cultural, biological and minimal chemical methods. Use resistant varieties and practice crop rotation to break disease cycles.

Biological Control

Use beneficial microbes (Trichoderma, Bacillus) to suppress soil-borne pathogens and encourage plant vigor.

Organic Amendments

Apply compost and green manures to improve soil structure, microbial health and root resilience.

Water & Drainage Management

Avoid waterlogging; practice drip irrigation and proper field leveling for reduced disease pressure.

Community Actions

  • Farmer field schools and peer training
  • Local disease monitoring groups and simple reporting
  • Seasonal awareness drives before sowing periods
