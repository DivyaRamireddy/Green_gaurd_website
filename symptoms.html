Common Early Symptoms

Yellowing leaves

Often due to nutrient deficiency or root disease.

Spots on leaves

Brown/black spots may indicate fungal or bacterial infection.

Powdery white coating

Typical of powdery mildew (fungus).

Wilting

If sudden, could be vascular wilt disease or root rot.

Stunted growth

Root damage, viruses, or poor soil health can cause this.

Mosaic patterns

Patchy light/dark areas often indicate viral infection.

Tip: Regular field checks and simple symptom photos can help detect problems early.
